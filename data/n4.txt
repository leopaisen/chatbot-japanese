Verb Conjugation
ている (Te iru): Continuous/progressive tense.
てある (Te aru): Resultative state.
ておく (Te oku): Doing something in advance.
てみる (Te miru): Trying something.
てしまう (Te shimau): Completing an action (often with a sense of regret).
そうです (Sou desu): It looks like (based on appearance).
ようです (You desu): It seems that (based on reasoning).
みたいです (Mitai desu): It seems like, looks like.
ことがある (Koto ga aru): Have done (past experience).
ことにする (Koto ni suru): Decide to do.
ことになる (Koto ni naru): It has been decided that.
Sentence Structures
と思う (To omou): Think that.
という (To iu): It is said that, called.
ようにいう (You ni iu): To tell someone to do something.
ようになる (You ni naru): To become able to.
ようにする (You ni suru): To try to.
つもり (Tsumori): Intend to.
ため (Tame): For, in order to.
ながら (Nagara): While doing.
たら (Tara): If, when (conditional).
なら (Nara): If, when, since.
ば (Ba): If (conditional).
と (To): When, if (conditional).
Particles and Connectives
ので (Node): Because, since.
のに (Noni): Despite, although.
だけ (Dake): Only.
しか (Shika): Only, nothing but.
でも (Demo): Even, something like.
など (Nado): Such as, and so on.
はず (Hazu): Supposed to, should be.
はずがない (Hazu ga nai): Cannot be.
べき (Beki): Should, ought to.
つもり (Tsumori): Plan to, intend to.
Comparisons and Degree
より (Yori): More than.
ほど (Hodo): To the extent.
一番 (Ichiban): The most.
方がいい (Hou ga ii): It's better to.
方がいいですか (Hou ga ii desu ka): Should I...?
Additional Grammar Points
かどうか (Ka dou ka): Whether or not.
ことができる (Koto ga dekiru): Can, be able to.
ということ (To iu koto): Means that.
というのは (To iu no wa): The thing that, as for.
ように (You ni): So that, in order to.
らしい (Rashii): It seems that, apparently.
みたい (Mitai): Like, similar to.
あげる (Ageru), くれる (Kureru), もらう (Morau): Giving and receiving verbs.
やすい (Yasui): Easy to.
にくい (Nikui): Difficult to.
すぎる (Sugiru): Too much, excessively.
かもしれない (Kamo shirenai): Might, may.
なければならない (Nakereba naranai): Must, have to.
なくてもいい (Nakute mo ii): Don't have to, it's okay not to.
