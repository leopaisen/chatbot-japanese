Verb Conjugation and Usage
させる (Saseru): Causative form.
られる (Rareru): Passive form.
させられる (Saserareru): Causative-passive form.
ていく (Te iku): To go on doing.
てくる (Te kuru): To come to do.
てほしい (Te hoshii): Want someone to do.
てもらう (Te morau): Receive the favor of someone doing something.
てくれる (Te kureru): Someone does something for the speaker.
てあげる (Te ageru): Do something for someone.
Sentence Structures and Clauses
ことにする (Koto ni suru): Decide to do.
ことにしている (Koto ni shite iru): Make it a rule to.
ようにする (You ni suru): Try to, make an effort to.
ようにしている (You ni shite iru): Be trying to, making an effort to.
ようになる (You ni naru): Come to be able to.
ように (You ni): So that, in order to.
のように (No you ni): Like, as if.
のような (No you na): Like, similar to.
かのように (Ka no you ni): As if.
かのような (Ka no you na): As if.
ことがある (Koto ga aru): There are times when.
こともある (Koto mo aru): There are also times when.
といい (To ii): It would be good if.
ばいい (Ba ii): It would be good if.
たほうがいい (Ta hou ga ii): It’s better to.
ないほうがいい (Nai hou ga ii): It’s better not to.

Comparisons and Degree
ほど (Hodo): To the extent that, so much that.
くらい (Kurai): To the extent that, so much that.
くらいなら (Kurai nara): If it’s to the extent that.
ほどなら (Hodo nara): If it’s to the extent that.
だらけ (Darake): Full of, covered with.
まみれ (Mamire): Covered with, stained with.
